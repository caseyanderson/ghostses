ghostses

a duo

each performer reads and has a batterie of instruments including:
  • an assortment of tuning forks (random pitches)
  • an am/fm radio
  • a harmonica or similar reed instrument
  • a kitchen timer/bell
  • a variety of little percussion instruments (rocks, toys, etc.)

the corpus

any text written by W.G. Sebald may be selected as the corpus. initial performances used Chapter 1 from the Rings of Saturn.

the corpus is separated into multiple files (layers) via a Python program. one layer contains all text (the background layer) and is printed on white paper while numerous foreground layers contain filtered, colored portions of the text (currently organized into parts of speech) printed on transparencies. performers assign a category from their batterie to each foreground layer (nouns refer to radio, verbs to tuning forks, etc.).

the stack(s)

a stack is created by combining (stacking) two or more foreground layers on top of a background layer (all from the same page). progression through each page (stack) is dictated by each performer's silent reading of the background text (normal reading rate [top to bottom, left to right], not/loosely coordinated).

the actions

performers execute an action when they arrive at a word on the background sheet that intersects with that same word on a foreground sheet, simultaneously reading that word aloud and up to one or two words directly proceeding and/or following (or both proceeding and following). reading volume and the volume of ones action should be generally balanced/nearly even.
